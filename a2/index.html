COVID-19 Visualisations 2020-2022

In the past few years, the COVID-19 epidemic has swept the world. This virus is long-lasting and highly contagious. Many people have not been spared. This epidemic has had a great impact on people's lives and health.


The following visualization shows the global impact of the epidemic during 2020-2022, recording in detail the number of infections and recoveries in each region.

Severity

This map shows how each continent has been affected by the COVID-19 epidemic during 2020-2022. Color is used to show the degree of impact(The gray area represents uncollected data).


We can see from the map that all parts of the world have been affected by the epidemic to varying degrees. Among them, countries such as, USA, Brazil, and India are among the hardest-hit areas with the largest number of infections.

Confirmed cases

The pie chart on the right presents the total number of infections in each continent between 2020-2022. We can see that European and Asian countries have the largest total cases, reaching an astonishing 190 million and 150 million, accounting for 37.3% and 28.8% of the global number of infections respectively.

Potential factors for the spread of the epidemic

Will the number of infected people increase as the population of a country increases? The Scatter plot above shows the relationship between the population and the number of confirmed cases in each country. As shown in the figure, although many countries with large populations, such as USA and India, have more confirmed cases. However, the population and the number of confirmed cases are not directly proportional, such as in China and some other countries.


Obviously, the number of confirmed cases does not only depend on the country's population, but may also be related to other environmental factors and people's awareness of prevention.

Successfully cured

Through people's unremitting efforts, the epidemic was finally brought under control. The bar chart on the right shows the COVID-19 cure rate in each continent. As you can see from the figure, the cure rates on all continents are above 84%. The countries with the highest cure rates are Australia/Oceania and North America, which have reached 93.21% and 95.17% respectively.

Fight the epidemic

The graph above is about illness and death per million people in different countries on different continents. In general, the morbidity and mortality rates in Europe, North America and South America are higher than those in Africa, Asia and Australia/Oceania. Among the three continents where the situation is relatively mild, the situation in Asian countries is relatively more serious.

Note: This data is only recorded from 2020-2-15 until 2022-05-14,data source from Website.
